Opportunity

An open therapeutic category worth building a company around.

50–60% of U.S. dogs

are overweight or obese. Excess weight reduces mobility, compresses lifespan, and drives chronic metabolic disease. Veterinarians see this every day.

16-year gap in the market

Dirlotapide (Slentrol) was voluntarily withdrawn in 2010. No new pharmacologic entrant has defined canine obesity care since. The category has no modern standard.

$42.4B in U.S. pet spending

APPA projects $42.4 billion in veterinary care and product sales for 2026. That is the market backdrop, not a TAM claim. The canine obesity slice is large and entirely unserved.

Discovery engine

Computational protein design instead of brute-force screening.

AlphaFold 3

Structure prediction for canine receptor targets. Every candidate is modeled against dog-specific biology before a single molecule is synthesized.

BindCraft + RFdiffusion

De novo binder design and structure-guided optimization. Hundreds of candidates generated and scored computationally in days, not months.

Species-specific from day one

Canine receptor homology, pharmacokinetics, and dosing constraints are built into the design loop. These are not human drugs repackaged for dogs.

Science

Human GLP-1 drugs proved the category. Treat builds the canine version.

Semaglutide and retatrutide validated peptide-based metabolic intervention in humans. The same receptor biology exists in dogs, but canine physiology is different enough that human drugs cannot simply be repurposed.

Defensibility

The moat is the platform.

One species focus. Repeatable computational peptide engine. Multiple shots on goal across obesity, lean-mass preservation, and combination therapy. No single-asset dependency.
