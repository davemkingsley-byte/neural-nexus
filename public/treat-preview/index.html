Opportunity

An open therapeutic category worth building a company around.

50–60% of U.S. dogs

are overweight or obese. Excess weight reduces mobility, compresses lifespan, and drives chronic metabolic disease. Veterinarians see this every day.

16-year gap in the market

Dirlotapide (Slentrol) was voluntarily withdrawn in 2010. No new pharmacologic entrant has defined canine obesity care since. The category has no modern standard.

$42.4B in U.S. pet spending

APPA projects $42.4 billion in veterinary care and product sales for 2026. That is the market backdrop, not a TAM claim. The canine obesity slice is large and entirely unserved.

Science

Human GLP-1 drugs proved metabolic medicine works. Treat applies that thesis to dogs.

Semaglutide and retatrutide validated peptide-based metabolic intervention in humans. The same receptor biology exists in dogs. But canine physiology, pharmacokinetics, and regulatory requirements are different enough that human drugs cannot simply be repurposed.

Treat designs molecules for canine targets from the start. Structure-based computational design using AlphaFold 3, BindCraft, and RFdiffusion compresses the path from concept to testable candidate and improves capital efficiency.

One species focus. One regulatory strategy. Repeatable computational peptide engine. Multiple shots on goal across obesity, lean-mass preservation, and combination therapy. No single-asset dependency.

Regulatory strategy

CNADA: a faster path built for naturally occurring disease.

Canine obesity is a naturally occurring condition, not an induced model. That qualifies Treat for a Conditional New Animal Drug Application, which can reach the market faster and with less capital than a traditional NADA. The company is designed to hit meaningful inflection points without carrying the cost structure of a human drug program.
