Opportunity

An open therapeutic category worth building a company around.

50–60% of U.S. dogs

are overweight or obese. Excess weight reduces mobility, compresses lifespan, and drives chronic metabolic disease. Veterinarians see this every day.

16-year gap in the market

Dirlotapide (Slentrol) was voluntarily withdrawn in 2010. No new pharmacologic entrant has defined canine obesity care since. The category has no modern standard.

$42.4B in U.S. pet spending

APPA projects $42.4 billion in veterinary care and product sales for 2026. That is the market backdrop, not a TAM claim. The canine obesity slice is large and entirely unserved.

Discovery engine

AI-powered drug design. Hundreds of candidates before a single molecule is synthesized.

Treat uses frontier computational biology to design peptide candidates in silico, predict binding affinity, and optimize for canine-specific targets. This compresses timelines, reduces capital requirements, and generates a defensible pipeline of purpose-built molecules.

🧬

Structure-first design

Every candidate is modeled against canine receptor biology before synthesis. No trial-and-error screening.

⚡

Speed and capital efficiency

Computational design compresses years of bench work into weeks of simulation. More candidates, less burn.

🧪

Purpose-built, not repurposed

These are not human drugs repackaged for dogs. Canine pharmacokinetics and dosing are in the design loop from day one.

Thesis

Human GLP-1 drugs proved the category. Treat builds the canine version.

Semaglutide and retatrutide created a $100B+ human obesity market. The same receptor biology exists in dogs. Treat is the first company building a canine-specific peptide platform to capture that opportunity.

Defensibility

The moat is the platform.

One species. One regulatory strategy. A repeatable computational engine that generates multiple drug candidates across obesity, lean-mass preservation, and combination therapy.
