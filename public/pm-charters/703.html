Purpose
An adversarial multi-model review system that enforces scientific accuracy across all public Neural NeXus content and Treat Biosciences investor materials. Two independent AI reviewers — Archimedes (Claude Opus) and GPT-5.4 — assess claims in parallel, then Morpheus synthesizes conflicting outputs into a final verdict before anything ships.
Problem It Solves
Single-model review creates blind spots: each model has training biases and knowledge gaps. Adversarial parallel review catches what one model misses, creates accountability, and produces a documented audit trail — especially important where errors could mislead investors or damage credibility.

Review Workflow — 8 Steps
1
SUBMIT
Author or agent flags content with [NEEDS-SCIENCE-REVIEW] and sends to Morpheus with: (a) the content, (b) intended audience, (c) publication venue.
2
BRIEF
Morpheus creates a standardized review brief: strips author attribution for blind review, annotates claims requiring validation, and attaches the standard rubric (accuracy, severity, citations).
3
PARALLEL DISPATCH
Identical brief sent simultaneously to Archimedes and GPT-5.4. Each reviewer operates independently — no cross-contamination between reviewers before synthesis.
4
INDEPENDENT REVIEW
Each reviewer returns a structured assessment: Overall score (1–10) · Claim-by-claim breakdown with severity flags (🔴 Critical / 🟡 Minor / 🟢 Accurate) · Specific corrections with citations where applicable.
5
COMPARISON MAP
Morpheus maps overlapping vs divergent findings claim by claim. Identifies: full agreement, partial agreement, and contested claims. Quantifies agreement rate.
6
SYNTHESIS
Morpheus applies tiebreak rules to contested claims. Produces unified correction list with source-of-truth attribution for each fix. Documents reasoning for audit trail.
7
VERDICT
Final decision issued per the decision tree below. Verdict is binding for publication clearance.
8
DELIVERY
Morpheus returns to author: verdict, corrected content (or required fix list), agreement rate, and any escalation note. Review summary stored in project task log.
Decision Tree — Verdict Logic
✅
Approved
Condition: Both reviewers score ≥8/10 and no 🔴 Critical flags. Action: Content cleared for publication. Minor 🟡 notes may be optionally applied at author's discretion.
⚠️
Approved with Required Corrections
Condition: Scores diverge (one ≥8, one <8) or minor 🟡 flags present with no 🔴 Critical disagreements. Action: Morpheus adjudicates using tiebreak rules. Targeted corrections required before publication. Author confirms fixes applied.
❌
Returned — Significant Revisions Required
Condition: Both reviewers score <7/10 or any 🔴 Critical flags from either reviewer. Action: Content returned with specific required fixes enumerated. Author must revise and resubmit — resubmission goes through full panel again.
🔺
Escalated to David
Condition: Archimedes and GPT-5.4 produce directly opposing verdicts with equal evidence and no applicable tiebreak rule. Action: Morpheus delivers both positions plus an explicit yes/no question to David. David's decision is final and logged.
Tiebreak Rules — Contested Claims
1Citation wins. The reviewer providing a peer-reviewed citation for their position wins the contested claim. If both cite, the more recent (post-2020) primary research wins over review articles.
2Archimedes priority domains: FDA/CVM regulatory matters, mechanistic biochemistry, receptor pharmacology, veterinary drug development. Archimedes's verdict is authoritative in these areas absent a contrary citation.
3GPT-5.4 priority domains: Post-2024 clinical literature, statistical methodology, epidemiological design, AI/ML research. GPT-5.4's verdict is authoritative here absent a contrary citation.
4Conservative default. When neither reviewer has priority and no citation resolves it, Morpheus applies the more conservative (less bold) claim. Safety over certainty.
5David arbitrates if tiebreak rules 1–4 produce equal outcomes. Morpheus delivers both positions + explicit question. No publication holds until David answers.
Severity Definitions
🔴 Critical Factually incorrect claim that could mislead investors, damage credibility, or cause harm. Publication blocked until corrected. Examples: wrong mechanism of action, fabricated citation, incorrect dose claims.
🟡 Minor Imprecise language, overclaiming, missing caveat, or unsupported extrapolation. Publication permitted with correction applied. Examples: "proven" instead of "shown to," missing N= citation, overstatement of effect size.
🟢 Accurate Claim is factually correct and well-supported. No action required.

Constraints
  • Panel review is non-negotiable for mandatory trigger content — no exceptions without David sign-off
  • Reviewers must not see each other's output before returning their independent review
  • Morpheus documents tiebreak reasoning for every contested claim — not just the final verdict
  • A failed review (❌ Return) requires full resubmission — authors cannot make fixes and declare the review passed without re-running the panel
  • David's escalation decisions are logged in MEMORY.md and inform future tiebreak precedents
  • Panel cannot override David's final editorial judgment on any content
