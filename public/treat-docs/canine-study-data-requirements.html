Executive Summary

This document defines the complete data package required to (1) conduct a clinical study in client-owned obese dogs and (2) submit a Conditional New Animal Drug Application (CNADA) to FDA CVM for TRT-001. The CNADA pathway — established by the Animal Drug Availability Act of 1996 (ADAA) — permits approval on a "reasonable expectation of effectiveness" standard, enabling commercial sales during the conditional period while full effectiveness studies are completed. This lower evidentiary threshold is the primary regulatory advantage of the CNADA route over a full NADA. From first INAD-supporting data to conditional approval is estimated at 18–24 months.

⚠️
Single Highest-Priority Regulatory Action: Request a pre-submission meeting with FDA CVM immediately. This is free ($0) and potentially saves months of rework. Submit request to ADE-PreSubMeeting@fda.hhs.gov. Allow 4–8 weeks from request to meeting date. See §1.3 for full prep guidance.

1.2 INAD Requirements — Investigational New Animal Drug (21 CFR Part 511)

An INAD exemption is required before conducting any clinical investigation in animals intended to support a CNADA submission.

When INAD IS Required
  • Any study in target species (dogs) where data will be submitted to FDA CVM [21 CFR §511.1(a)]
  • Import of investigational drug across state lines [21 CFR §511.1(c)]
  • Studies at multiple investigator sites [21 CFR §511.1(b)(4)(ii)]
When INAD is NOT Required
  • In vitro studies (cell lines, biochemical assays)
  • Exploratory, non-GLP rodent studies not intended for submission
  • Preliminary, non-GLP single-dose tolerance studies in beagles if solely for internal decision-making (confirm with regulatory counsel)

1.3 Pre-Submission Conference Process

FDA CVM offers free pre-submission meetings through the Division of Animal Drugs and Feeds. This is arguably the single highest-value regulatory action Treat can take, costing $0 and potentially saving months of rework.

How to Request
  • Submit written request to FDA CVM ONADE via the pre-submission meeting request form at fda.gov/cvm
  • Email: ADE-PreSubMeeting@fda.hhs.gov
  • Include: company name, product name/code, proposed indication, 1–2 page background summary, and 5–10 specific questions
  • Scheduling: Allow 4–8 weeks from request to meeting date
What to Prepare
  1. One-page product overview: TRT-001 structure, mechanism, proposed indication, peptide length (~39 aa), route (SC injection)
  2. Draft development plan: Phase 0 (computational) → Phase 1 (in vitro) → Phase 2 (preclinical) → Phase 3 (clinical) → CNADA
  3. Specific questions list (see below)

Questions to Ask at Pre-Submission Meeting:

  1. Is TRT-001 (~39 aa synthetic peptide) classified as a drug (FDA CVM) or a biologic (USDA CVB)?
  2. Does canine obesity qualify as a "minor use in a major species" for CNADA purposes under ADAA 1996? (Note: MUMS Act qualification is separate and unlikely — see mums-act-analysis.html)
  3. What is the minimum safety data package required for CNADA (is a 28-day repeat-dose study in beagles sufficient, or is a 90-day study required)?
  4. Will FDA CVM accept a non-GLP pilot PK study (4 dogs) followed by a GLP-compliant 28-day study, or must the PK study also be GLP-compliant?
  5. What constitutes "reasonable expectation of effectiveness" for a canine weight loss drug — will in vitro receptor engagement data plus published GLP-1 data in obese dogs (Dik et al. 2025, PMID 40436366) be sufficient without a formal pilot efficacy study?
  6. Are there specific FDA CVM GFIs that address safety pharmacology requirements for GLP-1 class agonists in dogs?
  7. What analytical methods does FDA CVM require for identity/purity of a ~39 aa modified peptide?

3.2 Inclusion Criteria
Obesity Definition (All Must Be Met)
  • BCS ≥ 7/9 on standardized 9-point scale [3]
  • Body weight ≥ 120% of breed-specific ideal body weight (IBW)
  • DEXA-confirmed fat mass ≥ 35% of total body mass at screening
Other Inclusion
  • Species: Canis lupus familiaris; all breeds acceptable

  • Weight: 5–50 kg (exclude toy breeds <5 kg)

Preferred breeds for recruitment (high obesity prevalence): Labrador Retriever, Beagle, Golden Retriever, Cocker Spaniel, Basset Hound, mixed breed.

3.4 Sample Size and Power Analysis

Primary Endpoint: % change in body weight from baseline at 12 weeks

Assumptions
  • Expected treatment effect: −10% body weight in TRT-001 group (conservative; based on liraglutide in obese Golden Retrievers, Dik et al. 2025; human retatrutide Phase 2: ~22–28% at max dose)
  • Expected placebo effect: −1 to −2% (mild caloric awareness effect in monitored animals)
  • Δ (group difference) = 8–10%
  • Standard deviation (σ) = 8–10% (based on intra-group variability from liraglutide studies in dogs [4])
  • Type I error (α) = 0.05 (two-sided) | Power (1-β) = 0.80
n per group = 2σ²(z₀.₀₂₅ + z₀.₂₀)² / δ²   where z₀.₀₂₅ = 1.96, z₀.₂₀ = 0.842, (z sum)² = 7.85
Scenario σ δ n per group n with 20% dropout
Conservative 10% 8% 24 30
✅ Base case (recommended) 8% 10% 13 16
Pessimistic 12% 8% 35 44
Recommended target enrollment: 30 dogs per group (60 total), providing ≥80% power under the conservative scenario with 20% dropout accommodation. Consistent with FDA CVM's expectation for "reasonable expectation of effectiveness" conditional approval. Stratify by weight class (5–15 kg, 16–30 kg, 31–50 kg) and sex; permuted block randomization.
3.5 Blinding

Double-blind: Owner, treating veterinarian, and outcome assessors are all blinded. Only the unblinded pharmacist/dispensary and data manager see treatment assignment. The placebo injection must be visually identical to TRT-001 — same formulation vehicle, same pH, same osmolality, same injection volume, same color. Requires a matched placebo manufacturing batch at the same CDMO.

Dose escalation rationale: GLP-1 agonist-induced GI side effects (nausea, vomiting) are the primary cause of early discontinuation. Incremental dose escalation — as used with semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) and liraglutide — significantly reduces GI adverse events. This is particularly critical in dogs, which vomit more readily than humans in response to GLP-1 class drugs.

Why DEXA is the Critical Differentiator: GLP-1 agonists in humans cause 25–40% of their total weight loss from lean mass (muscle) [5]. TRT-001's triple agonism — particularly the GCGR thermogenic component — may preferentially drive fat loss while preserving lean mass. DEXA-documented lean:fat mass ratio change distinguishes a weight-loss drug that causes cachexia from one that selectively reduces fat. This data will be pivotal for marketing positioning vs. any future single-agent GLP-1 competitor.

4. GMP Manufacturing for Clinical Supply

5.5 Record Retention

Per 21 CFR §514.3 and §514.80, CNADA records must be retained for the longer of: (a) 2 years after the drug is no longer marketed, or (b) 5 years after approval/conditional approval. INAD records per 21 CFR §511.1(d)(3) must be retained for the duration of the INAD + 5 years. Practical implication: Plan for ≥7 years minimum record retention from completion of the clinical study.

6.2 Eligibility Confirmation: "Minor Use in a Major Species"
Critical distinction: The "minor use" concept for CNADA eligibility under ADAA 1996 is DISTINCT from "minor use" under the MUMS Act of 2004. Under ADAA 1996, "minor use" is interpreted more broadly to include any indication for which there is no currently approved treatment. Because there are currently no FDA-approved drugs for canine obesity (Slentrol was withdrawn in 2010), canine obesity DOES qualify as a "minor use" under ADAA 1996 for CNADA purposes.
[IMPORTANT] Confirm this interpretation explicitly at the FDA CVM pre-submission meeting. See mums-act-analysis.html for full MUMS vs. ADAA 1996 analysis.
6.3 Annual Renewal Requirements

Under 21 CFR §514.1(b)(8)(ii), the conditionally approved drug must be renewed annually:

  1. Submit annual report demonstrating progress toward full effectiveness demonstration
  2. Include: status of ongoing effectiveness studies; any new safety information; manufacturing/quality update
  3. Renewal fee: ~$750–1,500/year (confirm current ADUFA rates)
  4. FDA CVM reviews renewal and either renews conditional approval or requires additional data

Maximum conditional approval period: 10 years (initial 5 years + one 5-year extension under ADAA 1996). At year 5: submit full effectiveness data converting to full NADA, apply for extension, or withdraw.

6.4 Generating Revenue During Conditional Approval
Primary Commercial Advantage: Product can be sold to licensed veterinarians (Rx drug) upon conditional approval. Revenue begins at approximately Month 30 from start (~Month 21 if faster execution). Veterinary drug prices are unregulated by FDA. The CNADA pathway enables Treat to generate revenue while still conducting full-scale effectiveness studies — dramatically changing the financing model vs. the 3–5 year pre-revenue period of a full NADA strategy.
