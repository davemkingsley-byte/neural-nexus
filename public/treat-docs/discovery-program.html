Section A Program Overview
Objective

Design, computationally optimize, and in vitro validate a panel of canine-specific triple incretin agonist peptide candidates targeting canine GLP-1R, GIPR, and GCGR simultaneously, leading to the selection of a single lead compound (designated TRT-001) for progression to preclinical development.

Scope

This program covers the full discovery phase: from target biology characterization and receptor structure acquisition through computational peptide design, in silico screening, variant panel synthesis, and in vitro functional validation. The program ends at Lead Candidate Selection (Gate 2): in vitro EC50 confirmed at all three canine receptors.

Preclinical development, IND-enabling studies, and regulatory submissions are addressed in companion documents.

Strategic Context

TRT-001 is designed to be the first canine-optimized triple incretin agonist, based on the retatrutide scaffold (LY3437943; Eli Lilly), targeting GLP-1R/GIPR/GCGR.

The canine obesity market represents ~54 million affected animals in the US with zero approved pharmacological treatments since dirlotapide (Slentrol) was withdrawn in 2010.

The discovery program is deliberately capital-efficient: ~$500 in cloud compute for the computational phase, $29–44K for in vitro validation.

Section B Target Biology and Rationale
B.1 — The Case for Triple Agonism

The evolution from single to triple incretin agonism represents a stepwise increase in weight loss efficacy that is mechanistically additive, not redundant:

Agent Targets Human Weight Loss Key Study
Semaglutide (Wegovy) GLP-1R ~15–17% STEP 1 trial [1]
Tirzepatide (Zepbound) GLP-1R + GIPR ~22–25% SURMOUNT-1 trial [2]
Retatrutide BEST IN CLASS GLP-1R + GIPR + GCGR 24.2% (24 mg, 24 wk) Jastreboff et al. 2023 [3]

The Phase 2 retatrutide data (PMID 37351564) are decisive: at 24 weeks, the 24 mg dose achieved 24.2% mean body weight reduction vs. placebo (p<0.001), with a dose-response relationship from 4 mg (8.7%) to 24 mg (24.2%). This represents the highest weight loss efficacy ever recorded for any drug in controlled clinical trials in humans.

Strategic Rationale: If designing de novo from scratch — as Treat Biosciences is doing — the marginal cost of engineering triple agonism versus dual agonism is negligible (sequence design complexity) while the potential efficacy gain is substantial (~5–8 percentage points of body weight).
B.2 — Receptor-Level Biology and Mechanism

GLP-1R: Appetite Suppression and Gastric Motility

GLP-1 (7-36)NH₂ is released from intestinal L-cells in response to nutrient ingestion and acts at: the hypothalamus/brainstem (reduces food intake via inhibition of AgRP/NPY, activation of POMC [4]), GI tract (slows gastric emptying), and pancreas (insulin secretion, glucose-dependent).

In dogs, GLP-1R preferentially drives glucose uptake into peripheral tissues rather than augmenting insulin secretion (Dik et al. 2013 [5]). Dik et al. 2025 (PMID 40436366) confirmed liraglutide at 1.2 mg/dog achieved significant reductions in body condition score, body weight, appetite, cholesterol, and triglycerides over 40 days in obese Golden Retrievers [6] — direct proof of concept.

Human GLP-1R (P43220) → Canine GLP-1R (A0A8I3NUR9): 89.6% overall identity; 13/13 mutagenesis-validated ligand contact residues 100% conserved [7,8]. The TMD activation pocket is functionally identical between species.

GIPR: Insulin Sensitization and Adipose Signaling

Pharmacological GIPR agonism — particularly the "superagonist" profile of retatrutide at GIPR (8.9× more potent than native GIP [9]) — has a net anti-obesity effect through adipose tissue remodeling and enhanced GLP-1R-mediated effects. Coskun et al. 2022 proposed that retatrutide's GIPR superagonism acts to desensitize the receptor in adipose tissue, shifting the balance away from lipid storage [9].

Human GIPR (P48546) → Canine GIPR (A0A8C0QLM4): 86.7% overall identity; ECL1 is 63.6% conserved (4/11 positions differ), while TM5 and TM6 are 100% conserved. ECL1 divergence is the primary optimization target for TRT-001.

GCGR: Thermogenesis and Hepatic Fat Oxidation

GCGR drives energy expenditure through hepatic fat oxidation (β-oxidation, anti-NAFLD), thermogenesis via brown adipose tissue (sympathetic nervous system), and mild ketogenesis during caloric restriction.

Critically: canine glucagon is 100% identical to human glucagon (zero amino acid differences), confirming evolutionary matching of selective pressure. This makes the GCGR component the highest-confidence component of TRT-001.

Human GCGR (P47871) → Canine GCGR (A0A8P0SBC0): 83.5% overall identity; 22/23 mutagenesis-validated contact residues conserved (95.7%). The single substitution, H339R at ICL3, is a conservative His→Arg change (both positively charged) at the G-protein coupling interface, NOT the orthosteric ligand binding site [10].

B.3 — Canine-Specific Pharmacology Differences
⚠️ Insulinotropic axis divergence: In dogs, GLP-1 drives glucose uptake via peripheral tissue mechanisms rather than primarily augmenting insulin secretion (Dik et al. 2013 [5]). For weight loss — primarily CNS and adipose tissue-mediated — this difference is not deleterious. However, dosing calibration must account for this.
Hepatic expression: Canine liver is rich in GCGR but has minimal GLP-1R and GIPR expression [11]. Hepatic metabolic effects (fat oxidation, anti-steatotic) are driven primarily by GCGR — given 100% identical glucagon sequences, this pathway translates directly.
⚠️ DPP-4 resistance and albumin binding: Canine DPP-4 substrate specificity is conserved with human, and retatrutide's Aib2 substitution prevents DPP-4 cleavage. Canine serum albumin is ~79% identical to human serum albumin — binding affinity may differ and requires empirical optimization (variant panel Section C.3) [12].

B.5 — Why the Retatrutide Scaffold

Retatrutide (LY3437943) is a 39-amino acid glucagon backbone peptide with His1-Aib2 N-terminus (DPP-4 resistance), Aib13 (additional stability and α-helical stabilization), K20 fatty diacid (C20 via γGlu-miniPEG₂ linker for albumin binding, t½ ~6 days in humans), and C-terminal GPSSGAPPPS extension from exendin-4 homology.

✅ Proven triple receptor engagement: Only validated triple incretin agonist with Phase 3 human data (ongoing as of 2026).
✅ Known SAR: Coskun et al. 2022 [9] and Xu et al. 2024 [13] provide full structure-activity relationship data and cryo-EM structure.
✅ Reduced design space: Starting from a validated scaffold reduces in silico design error; allows systematic canine-specific optimization.
✅ IP differentiation: Any canine-specific modifications (particularly GIPR ECL1 optimization) create genuine patentable novelty distinct from the parent retatrutide sequence.
Section C Computational Design Pipeline
Overview

The computational phase (~6 weeks, ~$500 compute cost) uses open-source and freely available academic tools to: acquire and validate target structures; map retatrutide contact residues onto canine receptor models; design 6–8 variant sequences optimized for canine receptors; screen variants in silico for binding energy, ADMET, and synthetic accessibility; and down-select to 6–8 candidates for synthesis.

Canine Receptor Structure Acquisition: AlphaFold3 Workflow

Step 1a: Download canine receptor sequences from UniProt: GLP-1R canine A0A8I3NUR9 (464 aa), GIPR canine A0A8C0QLM4 (466 aa), GCGR canine A0A8P0SBC0 (493 aa).

Step 1b: Submit each sequence to AlphaFold3 server (alphafoldserver.com) in FASTA format. Use full-length sequence including signal peptide.

Step 1c: For active-state GPCR conformation, perform peptide-receptor co-folding (canine receptor sequence + retatrutide). AlphaFold3 predicts binding pose and receptor conformation simultaneously. Predicted TM-score > 0.7 indicates high confidence [18]. Validate by superposition with human receptor PDB structure (expected RMSD < 1.5 Å at conserved positions).

Step 1d: For GIPR (primary optimization target), run additional molecular dynamics (MD) on the canine GIPR/retatrutide complex to understand ECL1 conformational dynamics.

Cost: $0 (free server) to $10 (Google Colab Pro) per structure batch.

C.3 — Step 3: Variant Design — Canine Optimization Strategy (~2 weeks)

Design Principles

Principle 1 — Minimize changes from retatrutide: Every deviation from the validated scaffold introduces uncertainty. Only make changes computationally justified by canine receptor divergence analysis. The Aib2, Aib13, and K20 fatty diacid must be retained in ALL variants.
Principle 2 — GIPR ECL1 is the primary target: The 4 ECL1 substitutions in canine GIPR create an altered binding surface for retatrutide residues 10–21. Canine-specific optimization at these positions is the primary design task.
Principle 3 — IP differentiation: Any canine-specific substitution that demonstrably improves potency at canine GIPR constitutes patentable novelty distinct from the retatrutide parent sequence. This is the "inventive step."
Principle 4 — Maintain DPP-4 resistance: Aib2 (position 2) MUST be retained in all variants. DPP-4 cleaves the His1-Ala2 bond of native GLP-1; Aib at position 2 sterically prevents this. Loss would reduce half-life by >90% in vivo.

C.4 — Step 4: In Silico Screening (~1 week)

Molecular Dynamics Simulations

  • Platform: OpenMM 8.x [20] (open source, GPU-accelerated)
  • Force field: CHARMM36m [21] for protein; GAFF2 for Aib and lipid modifications
  • System: Peptide-receptor complex embedded in POPC membrane bilayer
  • Simulation length: 10 ns minimum per complex (3 complexes per variant = 30 ns/variant)
  • GPU: 1× NVIDIA A100 or equivalent; ~1 hr per 10 ns on A100

Key readouts:

  • RMSD of peptide within binding pocket (want < 2 Å over 10 ns)
  • Number of stable hydrogen bonds at interface (want ≥ 8)
  • Interface buried surface area (want > 1200 Å²)
  • Binding free energy estimate via MM-GBSA
Cloud cost: ~$3/hr on Lambda Labs A100; ~30 hrs total = ~$90/variant; ~$720 total for 8 variants

Section E IP Strategy During Discovery

File BEFORE synthesis begins (~Week 6, end of computational phase). Cost: $2,500–5,000.

The provisional establishes priority date for all claims. The 12-month window allows synthesis and in vitro data to strengthen the non-provisional application. Target filing date: Week 6.
