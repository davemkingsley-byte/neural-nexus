01 Executive Summary

KEY FINDINGS — VERIFIED

This report presents a residue-level comparison of the three G-protein coupled receptors targeted by retatrutide (LY3437943) between Homo sapiens and Canis lupus familiaris. All substitutions are mapped against mutagenesis-validated ligand-contact residues from published cryo-EM structures and functional studies.

🔬 Primary finding: Across 36 mutagenesis-validated ligand-contact residues in GLP-1R (13) and GCGR (23), 35 are identical between human and dog. The single GCGR substitution (H339R, not H339Y as initially reported) maps to the G-protein coupling interface, not the peptide-binding site. The transmembrane domain (TMD) activation pocket is functionally conserved across all three receptors.

Conclusion: A retatrutide-based canine triple incretin agonist will engage all three target receptors with minimal or no sequence modification required for the core pharmacophore.

09 Limitations & Caveats
  1. All canine sequences are from TrEMBL (computationally predicted, PE=3), not Swiss-Prot reviewed. Gene prediction artifacts exist.
  2. GIPR has fewer published mutagenesis-mapped contact residues. Domain-level analysis is less definitive than residue-level.
  3. Sequence conservation ≠ identical pharmacology. Post-translational modifications, receptor expression levels, and signaling adapters may differ.
  4. Individual breed variation within C. lupus familiaris has not been assessed.
  5. Retatrutide C20 fatty diacid binds serum albumin for half-life extension. Canine serum albumin (~79% identity to human) may require separate lipidation optimization.
  6. ECL1 conformational dynamics cannot be fully predicted from sequence alone — molecular dynamics simulations recommended before finalizing peptide modifications.
