Section 2 Pharmacokinetics Study Design
2.1 — Rationale for PK-First Approach

PK data serve dual purposes:

  1. Dose selection for safety and efficacy studies: Without knowing exposure (AUC, Cmax), dosing in safety studies is guesswork. PK-first design prevents both underdosing (inadequate exposure → false negative safety) and overdosing (supratherapeutic exposure → avoidable adverse events).
  2. Half-life determination drives clinical dosing regimen: A t½ > 96h enables monthly dosing (analogous to Cytopoint, which is dosed every 4–8 weeks); t½ > 24h enables weekly dosing.

2.4 — Analyte and Analytical Method
  • Analyte: TRT-001 (intact peptide, primary)
  • Matrix: Plasma (EDTA-anticoagulated; separate and freeze at -80°C within 30 min of collection)
  • Method: LC-MS/MS — Column: C18 reverse-phase (Acquity UPLC Peptide BEH C18, 300 Å, 1.7 µm); Mobile phase A: 0.1% formic acid/water; Mobile phase B: 0.1% formic acid/acetonitrile; Detection: Triple quadrupole MS/MS
  • LLOQ target: ≤0.1 ng/mL
  • Internal standard: Stable isotope-labeled (SIL) TRT-001 analog (¹³C₆/¹⁵N₂-labeled; ~$3,000–5,000)
  • Sample volume: 100 µL plasma per timepoint
  • Method validation: Per FDA bioanalytical guidance (GFI #216 [1]) — accuracy, precision, selectivity, LLOQ, linearity, stability

2.6 — Dose Translation from In Vitro EC50
This section uses placeholder values pending Gate 2 data; update with actual EC50 values post-Gate 2.

Allometric scaling approach from in vitro to in vivo:

  1. Determine cAMP EC50 at canine GLP-1R in stable cell line (e.g., 1 nM)
  2. Account for protein binding: EC50_free = EC50_total / (1 − protein_binding); protein binding ~98–99% for lipidated peptide [2]
  3. Calculate plasma Cmin required: Cmin ≥ EC50_free × 2 (2× safety factor)
  4. Calculate dose: Dose (mg/kg) = Cmin × Vd / F

For reference: Semaglutide (0.5–2 mg/week SC in humans) achieves plasma Cmax of ~60–200 ng/mL; 2.4 mg Wegovy dose → ~200 ng/mL Cmax [3]. TRT-001 dosing likely in the 0.5–5 mg/dog/month range for a 10–30 kg dog — consistent with Cytopoint pricing model ($50–150/injection).

Section 3 Formulation Development
3.1 — Vehicle Selection

Subcutaneous peptide formulations require a vehicle that maintains peptide solubility and stability, is well-tolerated at SC injection site (minimal irritation, swelling), has physiological tonicity and acceptable pH (6.0–7.4 for SC), and supports shelf life of ≥24 months (target product profile).

Vehicle Composition pH Notes
Histidine buffer RECOMMENDED 10 mM L-histidine, 150 mM NaCl, 0.01% Tween 80 6.0 Used for semaglutide [4]; excellent stability for Aib peptides
Acetate buffer 10 mM sodium acetate, 140 mM NaCl, 0.02% Tween 80 4.0–5.0 Lower pH; may cause injection site discomfort
Phosphate buffer PBS + 0.02% Tween 80 7.4 Standard; may promote peptide aggregation at neutral pH
Trehalose + histidine 10 mM histidine, 9% trehalose, 0.01% Tween 80 6.0 Lyoprotectant; use for lyophilized formulation
Surfactant: Polysorbate 80 (Tween 80) at 0.01–0.02% prevents adsorption to vial surfaces and aggregation during agitation. Critical for long-acting peptides. Concentration target: 1–10 mg/mL for ≤1 mL injection volume.
3.2 — Lipidation Optimization for Canine Albumin

The C20 fatty diacid moiety (octadecandioic acid with γGlu-miniPEG₂ linker) of retatrutide binds human serum albumin (HSA) with Kd ~2–5 µM [2]. Canine serum albumin (CSA, ~79% identity to HSA) has an analogous fatty acid binding site but may differ in affinity.

Optimization approach:

  1. SPR measurement of TRT-001 variants vs. CSA (completed in in vitro phase, Assay 8)
  2. If any variant shows Kd (CSA) > 50 µM, test modified lipid anchors: C16 fatty acid (palmitic acid analog), C18 fatty acid (stearic acid analog), alternative linker length: miniPEG₁ vs. miniPEG₂ vs. miniPEG₃
  3. Select lipid variant with optimal CSA Kd for PK study
If CSA binding is insufficient: Consider adding a short canine-albumin-binding peptide sequence (ABD — albumin-binding domain) as C-terminal extension. This approach is more complex but provides species-specific albumin targeting.

Section 4 Safety Pharmacology
4.1 — Core Battery

The ICH S7A guideline (adapted for veterinary use by FDA CVM guidance) requires a core safety pharmacology battery before first clinical use. For a GLP-1/GIP/GCGR agonist, the core battery includes:

Cardiovascular Assessment

  • Continuous ECG telemetry in 4 conscious beagles (implanted telemetry devices)
  • Parameters: heart rate, PR interval, QRS duration, QT/QTc interval, blood pressure, body temperature
  • Duration: 24h baseline + 24h post-dose at therapeutic and 5× therapeutic dose
  • Key concern: GLP-1 agonists modestly increase heart rate in humans (~5 bpm) [5]; assess whether this effect translates to dogs and whether magnitude is clinically relevant
  • GCGR agonism may affect cardiac contractility (glucagon is used as antidote for beta-blocker overdose) — confirm no adverse cardiac chronotropic/inotropic effects at proposed doses

Respiratory Assessment

  • Whole-body plethysmography or head-out plethysmography in 4 conscious beagles
  • Parameters: respiratory rate, tidal volume, minute ventilation
  • Duration: 4h post-dose

CNS Assessment

  • Modified Irwin test (neurobehavioral battery) at 0, 2, 4, 8, 24h post-dose
  • Parameters: activity level, posture, tremor, reflexes, muscle tone
  • Note: GLP-1R is expressed in CNS; expected effects on food intake behavior should be distinguished from adverse CNS effects
4.2 — GI Tolerability Assessment (Critical for Canine Development)
⚠️ Dogs are more sensitive to GLP-1-induced nausea/vomiting than humans. This is a known class effect and the most likely dose-limiting adverse event for TRT-001. Comprehensive GI assessment is required at every preclinical stage.

Emesis Assessment in Beagles (6 animals at each dose level)

  • Place animals in individual kennels with video monitoring
  • Record emesis (retching + vomiting episodes) for 24h post-dose
  • Grade: Grade 0 (none), Grade 1 (single episode), Grade 2 (2–3 episodes), Grade 3 (≥4 episodes/distress)
  • Acceptable threshold: Grade 0–1 in ≥5/6 animals at therapeutic dose; Grade 2 at 3× therapeutic; Grade 3 only at >5× therapeutic

Dose-Escalation Strategy to Minimize GI Side Effects

  • Initiate dosing at 10% of projected therapeutic dose
  • Escalate by 50% increments every 2 weeks (analogous to semaglutide titration schedule in humans)
  • If vomiting occurs in >50% of animals at any dose level, hold at previous level for an additional 2 weeks before re-escalating
  • Record and document all GI events per FDA CVM adverse event definitions
GI motility assessment: Radiographic GI transit time at baseline and therapeutic dose (gastric emptying delay expected; quantify). This data supports dose-escalation scheme in clinical protocol.

5.4 — Thyroid C-Cell Note
In rodents, GLP-1R agonists cause thyroid C-cell hyperplasia and adenoma (medullary thyroid carcinoma risk) at high doses [6]. This is a class effect that drove the FDA boxed warning for semaglutide and liraglutide in humans. In dogs, GLP-1R expression in thyroid C-cells is lower than in rodents, and this finding is considered less clinically relevant [7]. Nevertheless, FDA CVM will require thyroid histopathology to rule out a class effect in canine species.

Assess: C-cell number, staining intensity (calcitonin IHC), presence of hyperplasia or neoplasia.

6.2 — DEXA Scanning Protocol

Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DEXA) is the gold-standard method for body composition assessment in dogs. Available at most university veterinary hospitals.

Measurements

  • Lean body mass (kg and % total weight)
  • Fat mass (kg and % total weight)
  • Bone mineral density and content (secondary)
  • Regional analysis: trunk, limbs, head

Why DEXA Matters for TRT-001 Specifically

GLP-1 agonists in humans cause 25–40% of their total weight loss from lean mass (muscle) [8]. This is a known limitation. TRT-001's triple agonism (particularly the GCGR thermogenic component) may preferentially drive fat oxidation and preserve lean mass. DEXA data demonstrating fat-selective loss vs. lean mass preservation would be a key differentiating feature vs. single GLP-1 agonists, and potentially strengthens the case for the v2 ActRIIB co-treatment pipeline.

Schedule

  • DEXA at baseline, Week 4, Week 8, Week 12
  • Weight and BCS: Weekly
  • Metabolic panel (fasted glucose, insulin, lipid panel): at baseline, Week 4, Week 12
6.3 — Why Body Composition Data Strengthens the CNADA Application

FDA CVM's conditional approval standard is "reasonable expectation of effectiveness" — not full proof of efficacy. Body composition data from 3–5 dogs showing:

  • Measurable body weight reduction (e.g., ≥3% at 8 weeks in dogs not on caloric restriction)
  • Favorable lean mass preservation ratio

...constitutes compelling preliminary efficacy evidence that, combined with in vitro receptor engagement data and the published literature on GLP-1 efficacy in dogs (Dik et al. 2025 [9]), satisfies the "reasonable expectation" standard. This data also provides effect size estimates for the power analysis in the clinical study design.

Section 8 INAD Filing Requirements
8.1 — What Is an INAD?

An Investigational New Animal Drug (INAD) exemption (21 CFR Part 511) is required before conducting any clinical investigation in animals with TRT-001. The INAD establishes a formal regulatory relationship with FDA CVM and is required for:

  • Any clinical study in animals intended to support a future NADA or CNADA
  • Import/export of investigational drug across state lines for clinical use
An INAD is NOT required for basic research, in vitro studies, or studies in laboratory rodents. The preclinical beagle studies described above would typically be conducted under INAD if intended to support regulatory submissions; however, exploratory non-GLP PK studies in beagles can sometimes proceed without INAD if truly exploratory and results will not be submitted to FDA. Confirm with regulatory consultant at the time.

Section 9 Go/No-Go Criteria — Gate 3
9.1 — Gate 3 Definition

All three criteria must be met to advance to clinical development (CNADA pathway):

✅ Criterion 1: Pharmacokinetic Adequacy

• SC bioavailability F ≥ 50% (measured in PK study)

• t½ ≥ 24h (minimum for weekly dosing) — target: t½ ≥ 96h for monthly dosing

• Cmax/EC50 ratio ≥ 5 at therapeutic dose (adequate therapeutic index on PK basis)

✅ Criterion 2: Safety Tolerance

• NOAEL ≥ 3× projected therapeutic dose in 28-day repeat-dose study

• No serious adverse events (SAEs) attributable to TRT-001 at therapeutic dose

• GI tolerability: Grade ≤2 emesis in ≤2/4 animals at therapeutic dose

• No histopathological findings of concern in liver, pancreas, or thyroid at NOAEL

• No clinically significant changes in CBC or chemistry at therapeutic dose

✅ Criterion 3: Clinical Signal (Exploratory)

• ≥2/5 obese dogs show ≥3% body weight reduction in 8-week pilot (or measurable change in any direction consistent with GLP-1 mechanism)

Note: This criterion is observational; pilot is underpowered for statistical significance. Any measurable signal strengthens CNADA application.
