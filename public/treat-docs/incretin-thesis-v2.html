Phase 2 data showed 24–28% body weight loss (vs ~15% for semaglutide, ~22% for tirzepatide). It hasn't been approved yet. Nobody is developing anything like it for dogs.

We will build the first canine-optimized triple incretin agonist. Not "Ozempic for dogs" — that's already one generation behind. This is "Retatrutide for dogs" before retatrutide exists in any species commercially.

Activin receptor blockade (muscle preservation) is our v2 pipeline asset — valuable, de-risked by Lilly's bimagrumab + tirzepatide trials, but not required for the lead product.

03 The Science

Why Triple Agonism, Not Single or Dual

Approach Human Drug Weight Loss Mechanism
GLP-1 alone Semaglutide (Wegovy) ~15–17% Appetite suppression, gastric slowing
Dual GLP-1/GIP Tirzepatide (Zepbound) ~22–25% Enhanced insulin sensitization, adipose signaling
Triple GLP-1/GIP/GCGR Retatrutide (Lilly Phase 3) ~24–28% Adds thermogenesis, hepatic fat oxidation

The glucagon receptor (GCGR) component is the differentiator. It adds energy expenditure via hepatic fat oxidation and thermogenesis — you're not just suppressing appetite, you're burning fat through a second pathway. This is why retatrutide showed the highest weight loss numbers ever recorded in clinical trials.

If we're designing de novo, the additional complexity of a triple agonist is primarily in peptide sequence design, not manufacturing. Same synthesis process, same injection, same COGS.

All four target receptors are conserved between human and dog. Analysis verified against canonical sequences with mutagenesis-validated contact residues.

Key Finding: The TMD activation pocket — where the retatrutide N-terminus drives receptor signaling — is 100% conserved at validated contact positions across GLP-1R and GCGR. GCGR has only one contact substitution (H339R, ICL3, G-protein face — not ligand pocket). Conservative (both positively charged). GIPR ECL1 is 63.6% conserved — this is where retatrutide's middle region makes receptor-specific contacts. GIPR is the primary optimization target.
Canine glucagon is identical to human glucagon (zero amino acid differences) — confirming matched selective pressure on GCGR and validating the GCGR binding site conservation data.

07 Development Path

Figure 4 — Development Timeline to Conditional FDA Approval
Four-phase development path from computational design through clinical trial and conditional FDA CVM approval (CNADA). Total capital requirement ~$500K across all phases. CRO recommendation for Phase 2–3: Texas A&M CVM (30–50% cheaper than commercial CROs, local to David). Primary endpoint: ≥10% body weight reduction with <20% lean mass loss.

Phase Details

Phase 0 — Computational Design (Weeks 1–6, ~$500)
Design triple incretin peptide variants based on retatrutide scaffold. Optimize GIPR ECL1 engagement using canine receptor model (AlphaFold2/3). ADMET prediction and down-selection. File provisional patent on lead sequences.
Phase 1 — Synthesis & In Vitro (Months 2–4, ~$29–44K)
Synthesize 6 peptide variants (see variant panel in conservation report). Build canine GLP-1R, GIPR, GCGR stable cell lines (or source from CRO). cAMP accumulation dose-response at all three canine receptors. Binding affinity assays. Gs/Gi BRET assay for GCGR (H339R bias check). Deliverable: Lead compound with measured EC50 at all three canine receptors.
Phase 2 — Preclinical (Months 4–8, ~$100–200K)
PK study: half-life, bioavailability in dogs. Canine albumin binding (lipidation optimization if needed). Single-dose safety in healthy dogs (3–5 dogs). Body composition (DEXA) signal in obese dogs. CRO recommendation: Texas A&M CVM (local to David, 30–50% cheaper than commercial CROs).
Phase 3 — Clinical (Months 8–18, ~$300–500K)
Conditional approval study in client-owned obese dogs (20–40 dogs). 12-week treatment: body weight, DEXA body composition, metabolic panel. Primary endpoint: ≥10% body weight reduction with <20% lean mass loss. → CNADA (Conditional New Animal Drug Application) to FDA CVM.

09 Open Questions

  • 1GIPR ECL1 optimization — Molecular dynamics or AlphaFold modeling needed to design canine-specific contacts. This is the primary computational task.
  • 2Canine albumin binding — Retatrutide's C20 fatty diacid binds human serum albumin for half-life. Canine albumin is ~79% identical. May need optimization.
  • 3GI tolerability — Dogs vomit easily. GLP-1 agonists cause nausea in humans. Dose-escalation protocol critical.
  • 4Delivery format — Injectable (simplest), implant (differentiated), or oral (hardest but highest value).
  • 5Will Zoetis/Elanco develop canine incretins internally? — Possible but big pharma moves slowly. First data wins.
  • 6Activin blockade approach — Peptide vs mAb vs skip at launch. Decision deferred to post-Phase 1 data and Lilly Phase 2 results (Jan 2027).
