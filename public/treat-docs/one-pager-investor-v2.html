The Problem

60% of US dogs — 54 million animals — are overweight or obese.

Zero FDA-approved weight loss drugs on the market. The last one (Slentrol) was pulled by Pfizer in 2010 — 16 years ago.

  • Obesity reduces canine lifespan by 2.5 years
  • #1 health concern among veterinarians
  • Dog owners spend $7,000+/year on pet care
  • Cytopoint (itching drug) earns $800M+/yr — proving price tolerance
  • Dec 2025 paper (PMID 40882685) confirms 16-year unmet need
The Solution

Treat Biosciences is developing TRT-001: a single injectable peptide targeting GLP-1R, GIPR, and GCGR — the same three receptors as Eli Lilly's retatrutide.

Retatrutide is the most potent weight-loss drug ever tested in humans: 24–28% body weight loss in Phase 2 (vs ~15% for semaglutide/Wegovy).

We're designing it from scratch, canine-optimized, before anyone else does it in any species commercially.

Route: Subcutaneous injection, monthly. COGS: $2–5/dose at scale.

Why It Works
  • 100% of mutagenesis-validated binding contacts conserved at canine GLP-1R
  • 95.7% binding pocket conservation at canine GCGR
  • Human GLP-1 drugs already work in dogs (Dik et al. 2025, Res Vet Sci, PMID 40436366)
  • Canine glucagon is identical to human glucagon (zero aa differences)
  • GIPR ECL1 (63.6% conserved) = primary optimization target — our inventive step
  • FTO clear — no blocking patents for canine peptide incretins
  • Triple agonism: appetite suppression + insulin sensitization + thermogenesis
