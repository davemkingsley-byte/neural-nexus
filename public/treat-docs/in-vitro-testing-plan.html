CONFIDENTIAL GATE 2
1 Objective

Measure binding affinity and functional activity (Gs-mediated cAMP accumulation as the primary readout) of TRT-001 variant peptides at canine GLP-1R, GIPR, and GCGR expressed in stable cell lines. Secondary objectives: characterize β-arrestin recruitment bias, signaling fidelity at the GCGR H339R variant, and pharmacokinetic surrogate markers (plasma stability, DPP-4 resistance, albumin binding).

Primary Decision (Gate 2)

Identify ≥1 TRT-001 variant with:

GLP-1R
EC50 < 10 nM
GIPR
EC50 < 50 nM
GCGR
EC50 < 50 nM

In canine stable cell lines → designate as lead compound for preclinical development.

1
cAMP Accumulation Assay PRIMARY READOUT
Gs-Coupled

Purpose: Measure Gs-coupled receptor activation. All three target receptors (GLP-1R, GIPR, GCGR) signal predominantly through Gs → adenylyl cyclase → cAMP production. This is the primary pharmacological readout.

Platform: HTRF cAMP assay (Cisbio/Revvity); or LANCE Ultra cAMP (PerkinElmer); or BRET2 cAMP sensor (CAMYEL construct).
Format: 5,000–10,000 cells/well in 384-well plate; 1-hour stimulation at 37°C; dose-response (10-point, 0.01 nM – 10 µM). 4-parameter logistic curve fitting (GraphPad Prism 10).

ReceptorTarget EC50 (TRT-001)Comparator Reference
Canine GLP-1R < 10 nM (ideal); < 100 nM (minimum) Semaglutide at human GLP-1R: ~0.05 nM; liraglutide at canine GLP-1R: ~1–5 nM [2]
Canine GIPR < 50 nM (ideal); < 200 nM (minimum) Native GIP at human GIPR: ~0.1 nM; retatrutide at human GIPR: ~0.011 nM
Canine GCGR < 50 nM (ideal); < 200 nM (minimum) Native glucagon at human GCGR: ~0.05 nM; canine GCGR expected similar
GIPR threshold justification: Retatrutide's 8.9× GIPR superagonism provides significant potency headroom. Even if canine ECL1 divergence reduces potency by 10×, TRT-001 may achieve EC50 ~1 nM at canine GIPR — comparable to or better than native GIP at human GIPR.

Radioligand Binding Assay — Kd and Ki
Binding Affinity

Purpose: Determine equilibrium binding affinity (Kd for saturation binding; Ki for competition binding). Required to distinguish potency differences from efficacy differences — partial agonists may show low cAMP EC50 but low Emax.

  • Option A (saturation binding): [¹²⁵I]-GLP-1, [¹²⁵I]-GIP, [¹²⁵I]-glucagon (or tritiated analogs)
  • Option B (TR-FRET-based — no radioactivity): Tb-chelate-labeled peptide + SNAP-tag receptor
  • Ki calculation: Cheng-Prusoff equation [3]
Readout
Ki (nM)
Cost Estimate
$3,000–5,000
Threshold
Ki < 100 nM (all 3)
3
β-Arrestin Recruitment Assay — Bias Profiling
Signaling Bias

Purpose: Characterize signaling bias. For canine tolerability (dogs vomit easily with GLP-1 agonists), understanding β-arrestin bias is critical. A Gs-biased profile may reduce GI side effects.

  • BRET-based: NanoLuc-tagged β-arrestin2 + receptor-Venus BRET pair
  • PathHunter (DiscoverX/Eurofins): β-arrestin enzyme fragment complementation (EFC) — most widely used for GPCR bias profiling
  • Bias factor: Log(τ/KA) from operational model of agonism [4]; compare cAMP vs. β-arrestin curves
If a TRT-001 variant shows Gs-biased profile, this is a differentiating feature that should be emphasized in IP and marketing positioning — potentially superior GI tolerability vs. semaglutide.
Platform
BRET / PathHunter

Calcium Flux Assay — GIPR Gq Coupling Check
Off-Target Check

Purpose: GIPR has documented Gq/Gi coupling in certain cell contexts in addition to primary Gs signaling [6]. This assay determines whether TRT-001 activates any calcium-mobilizing pathways in canine GIPR cells.

Platform: Fluo-4 AM or FLIPR Calcium 6 dye (Molecular Devices); FLIPR Tetra (384-well) or standard plate reader with fluorescence kinetics. 60-second read after compound addition; peak fluorescence vs. baseline.

Expected result: Minimal calcium flux (GIPR is predominantly Gs-coupled; any significant calcium signal warrants investigation).

5
GCGR Gs/Gi BRET Assay — H339R Signaling Fidelity
Species Validation

Purpose: The canine GCGR H339R substitution maps to ICL3, at the G-protein coupling interface. Confirm this substitution does not introduce Gi coupling or alter the Gs/Gi ratio. Gi signaling at GCGR would reduce intracellular cAMP and blunt the desired metabolic effect (thermogenesis, hepatic fat oxidation).

  • BRET2-based: RLuc8-tagged Gαs (or Gαi) + GFP2-tagged Gβγ
  • Mini-Gs/Mini-Gi recruitment BRET: SmBiT-tagged receptor + LgBiT-tagged mini-Gs or mini-Gi
  • Expected: Gs-dominant coupling ratio (Gs BRET/Gi BRET > 10:1), comparable to human GCGR

6
Canine Plasma Stability
PK Surrogate

Purpose: Determine metabolic stability of TRT-001 variants in canine biological matrix. Plasma stability predicts in vivo half-life and identifies labile bonds beyond DPP-4 cleavage.

  • Matrix: Fresh canine plasma (citrate-anticoagulated; Lampire Biological Laboratories or BioIVT)
  • Incubation: 37°C, 5% CO₂; timepoints: 0, 1, 4, 8, 24h
  • Analysis: LC-MS/MS with stable isotope-labeled internal standard; monitor parent peptide and major metabolites
Expected result based on retatrutide analogy: Aib2 modification provides DPP-4 resistance; Aib13 + fatty acid provide additional stability. Expected plasma t½ > 24h for lipidated variants (retatrutide t½ in humans: ~6 days [7]).
Analysis
LC-MS/MS
Cost Estimate
$2,000–4,000
Threshold
≥ 70% intact at 24h

DPP-4 Resistance
Confirmatory

Purpose: Explicitly confirm that the Aib2 modification protects TRT-001 variants from canine DPP-4 cleavage. This is a confirmatory assay — DPP-4 resistance should be robust given the Aib2 modification, but species-specific verification is required.

  • Enzyme: Recombinant canine DPP-4; or porcine DPP-4 as surrogate (96% identical to canine)
  • Timepoints: 0, 15, 30, 60, 120 min at 37°C
  • Positive control: GLP-1(7-36) without Aib2 (rapid DPP-4 cleavage expected; validates assay)

Canine Serum Albumin Binding (SPR) — Half-Life Predictor
SPR / ITC

Purpose: The C20 fatty diacid moiety at K20 binds serum albumin for half-life extension. Canine serum albumin (CSA) is ~79% identical to human serum albumin (HSA). The binding affinity must be empirically measured to select the optimal lipid moiety.

  • SPR: Biacore T200 or S200; CSA (Sigma-Aldrich A3803) immobilized on CM5 chip; TRT-001 variants as analytes. Full kinetic profile: kon, koff, Kd.
  • Alternative: Isothermal Titration Calorimetry (ITC); or equilibrium dialysis with [¹⁴C]-labeled peptide (lower cost, lower information)

5 Go/No-Go Criteria — Gate 2

Gate 2 Definition

✅
FULL GO: ≥1 variant meets EC50 < 10 nM (GLP-1R) AND EC50 < 50 nM (GIPR) AND EC50 < 50 nM (GCGR), Emax ≥ 70% native ligand, ≥ 70% plasma stability at 24h, no GCGR Gi bias. → Designate as TRT-001 lead; move to preclinical PK/safety study.
⚠️
PARTIAL GO (A): Best variant meets GLP-1R and GCGR criteria but GIPR EC50 in 50–500 nM range. → Consider dual GLP-1R/GCGR agonist as fallback. Commission additional 2–3 GIPR-optimized variants. Additional cost: $6,000–10,000; timeline: +4–6 weeks.
⚠️
PARTIAL GO (B): All three receptors active but EC50 in 100–1,000 nM range. → Enter lead optimization round. Focus on GIPR optimization + albumin binding adjustment. Re-screen 4–6 optimized variants.
❌
NO-GO: No variant with EC50 < 1 µM at any receptor. → Pause; reassess scaffold choice. Consider de novo design using RFdiffusion. Were accessions correct? Is computational model valid?
